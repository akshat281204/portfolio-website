Hyperface Technologies

SDE Intern Role

8:30 AM to 11:00 PM, 6th August, 2025

Hyperface was the third company to visit our campus since the placement season started. A total of 500 candidates appeared for the process, and only 3 (including me) were selected as interns. The entire process had five rounds and all of them were conducted in a single, grueling day.

Honestly, the procedure was very long and tiringâ€”from 8:30 AM to 11:00 PMâ€”but it gave me a lot of confidence. This was one of the very few procedures that didnâ€™t leave much scope for people to cheat their way in, though some still tried and were eventually caught.

Round Details:

  • Round 1 (MCQs): This had 20 questions. The first 10 were aptitude-based, and the rest were technical, focusing on core concepts, coding, Devops, and Linux. 140 candidates were selected for the next round.
  • Round 2 (DSA): This was a tense, non-IDE based round. We had 4 sets, each having 2 questions to be done under 30 minutes, and we had to write the code down in Notepad without any internet access. The questions were typical DSA: one based on strings (finding the longest common prefix) and the other based on trees (printing the right side view of a tree). 40 candidates were selected after this.
  • Round 3 (Machine Coding): This was my first time seeing a machine coding round for an SDE 1 role, but I tried to ignore that and focus. We were given a banking scenario with various classes and methods to code within 50 minutes. I chose Java since I was most comfortable implementing the necessary OOP concepts with it. 10 candidates made it to the interviews.
  • Round 4 (Technical Interview 1 - Coding Session): This felt more like a one-on-one coding session. After a brief introduction and some questions about my projects, the interviewer jumped straight to LeetCode. I had to solve a Graph question that required me to return all nodes reachable from node 0 without visiting a restricted node. I made sure to think out loud the entire time, and the interviewer constantly asked for justifications or suggested changes. I managed to solve it in around 20-25 minutes. The whole interview was about an hour long. 5 candidates were selected for the final round.
  • Round 5 (Technical Interview 2 - Core Concepts): This was the final round with two people interviewing me. They concentrated more on DBMS and OOP, which made sense given it's a fintech company. The initial questions were easy-to-medium, but the scenarios became increasingly complex. They also questioned me about tools I mentioned on my resume like Jenkins and Neo4j. Following that, I asked for feedback and how I could improve, which led to a good 10-15 minute discussion. This interview also lasted about an hour.

Following this final round, they announced the results, and I was one of the 3 candidates selected!

MathCo (Earlier as The Math Company)

Trainee Analyst Role

3rd September - 15th September, 2025

MathCo was the next company I was eligible for after securing my first offer, due to the college's two-offer policy (one above 6 LPA, one below). The process began with two tests on 3rd September, and the interviews followed on 15th September. A total of 600 candidates appeared for the initial test.

Round Details:

  • Round 1 (Aptitude & Guesstimates): This test included aptitude, pseudocode, and guesstimate questions. The difficulty was easy; if you've covered the topic once, you could answer the questions that were asked. (300 candidates were selected for the next round)
  • Round 2 (SpeechX): This was primarily a communication test with situational questions. We were asked to speak regarding a certain situation for 2 minutes and had only one chance. It ended with 30 easy MCQs on communication. (120 candidates were selected for the next round)
  • Round 3 (Technical Interview): This 30-minute interview started with an introduction and a detailed explanation of a confident project. The interviewer asked me to write down some basic SQL queries, followed by a scenario-based question on choosing a tech stack for a product. Finally, I was asked to write a Flask API.
  • Round 4 (Case Study): This round had 30 minutes for 5 questions. It included two easy DSA questions, a graphical question requiring analysis and outcome prediction, a probability-based question, and one guesstimate question.
  • Round 5 (HR Interview): This was the final round. I was questioned about my answers from the Case Study round and, crucially, how I was different from the other candidates sitting for the process.

Finally, they announced that 21 candidates were selected, and I was one of them.
